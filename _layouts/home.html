Hi there, I'm James.

I'm a PhD candidate at the University of Toronto in the Department of Medical Biophysics. I study cancer predisposition in Li-Fraumeni syndrome alongside a talented cross-disciplinary team at the Malkin lab in the SickKids Research Institute.

As a trained cell biologist, I've been using CRISPR technology to understand how mutations in the TP53 gene can initiate cancer development, and explore the effects of restoring normal p53 function in cell-based models.

Outside of the lab, I analyze data for BenchSci's machine learning platform, and help clients in biotechnology and pharmaceutical fields as a contract science consultant for Bloom Burton & Co.
